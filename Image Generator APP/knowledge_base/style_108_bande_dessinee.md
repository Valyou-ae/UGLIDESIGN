# STYLE 108: BANDE DESSINÉE (EUROPEAN COMIC)

## Overview
Franco-Belgian comic tradition featuring the "ligne claire" (clear line) technique, realistic proportions, detailed backgrounds, and watercolor-style coloring. Known for literary sophistication and artistic craftsmanship.

## Key Characteristics
- **Ligne Claire**: Uniform line weight, no heavy inks
- **Realistic Proportions**: Anatomically accurate
- **Detailed Backgrounds**: Architectural precision
- **Watercolor Aesthetics**: Soft, blended coloring
- **Expressive Faces**: Realistic but characterful
- **European Architecture**: Period-accurate details
- **Clean Panels**: Organized, readable layouts
- **Literary Quality**: Sophisticated storytelling

## Technical Parameters

### Line Work (Ligne Claire)
- **Weight**: Consistent 0.5-1.0mm throughout
- **Quality**: Clean, precise, no variation
- **Style**: Clear, legible, democratic (all lines equal)
- **No Heavy Inks**: No thick outlines or shadows
- **Precision**: Architectural drafting quality

### Coloring Technique
- **Watercolor Effect**: Soft, organic blending
- **Flat Colors with Subtle Shading**: Not cel-shaded
- **Muted Palette**: European sensibility
- **Realistic Light**: Natural lighting behavior
- **Texture**: Slight paper texture visible

### Background Detail
- **Architecture**: Period-accurate buildings
- **Perspective**: Technically correct
- **Details**: Street signs, windows, cobblestones
- **Depth**: Multiple planes of information
- **Research-Based**: Historically accurate

- Maintain consistent style

## Historical Context
Bande Dessinée emerged in early 20th century Europe, with Belgian and French publishers creating a distinct tradition separate from American comics. Hergé's Tintin (1929) revolutionized the medium with ligne claire. Post-WWII, BD matured into sophisticated literary form. 1960s-70s saw explosion of adult-oriented albums. The tradition influenced global comics and maintains prestigious cultural status in Europe today.

## Cultural Significance
- **The Ninth Art**: Recognized art form in France/Belgium
- **Album Format**: Hardcover 48-page standard
- **Literary Respect**: Studied in universities
- **Angoulême Festival**: Major international comic festival
- **Museum Recognition**: Permanent collections
