# 168. 1990s GRUNGE FASHION

### **Core Characteristics:**
- **Silhouette**: Loose, layered, anti-fashion
- **Aesthetic**: Deliberately sloppy, thrift store, authentic
- **Philosophy**: Anti-establishment, anti-consumerism
- **Rebellion**: Against 80s excess and corporate culture

### **The Grunge DNA:**

**Core Philosophy:**
```
Anti-Fashion Stance:
- Rejection of glamour
- Thrift store sourcing
- Worn, distressed items
- "I don't care" attitude
- Authenticity over appearance
- Comfort over style
- Gender-neutral approach

Visual Impact:
- Disheveled, unkempt
- Layered (hiding body)
- Muted, dark colors
- Minimal effort appearance
- Underground, alternative
```

### **Essential Grunge Elements:**

**The Flannel Shirt:**
```
Characteristics:
- Plaid pattern (usually)
- Oversized fit
- Worn open over band t-shirt
- Tied around waist
- Faded, soft from washing
- Often thrifted
- Red and black plaid iconic

Wearing Methods:
- Over t-shirt (unbuttoned)
- Around waist (grunge belt)
- Alone (buttoned once or twice)
- Sleeves rolled up
- Always oversized
```

**Band T-Shirts:**
```
Essential Bands:
- Nirvana (most iconic)
- Pearl Jam
- Soundgarden
- Alice in Chains
- Mudhoney
- Sonic Youth

Characteristics:
- Vintage, worn
- Oversized or fitted
- Often layered under flannel
- Sometimes cut or distressed
- Authentic (not reproductions)
```

**Ripped Jeans:**
```
The Sacred Denim:
- Levi's 501 or similar
- Light to medium wash preferred
- Ripped at knees (naturally worn)
- Frayed hems
- Loose fit
- Often dirty or stained
- Genuine wear, not manufactured
```

### **Layering System:**

**The Classic Grunge Layer:**
```
Layer 1: Band t-shirt or thermal
Layer 2: Flannel shirt (open)
Layer 3: Thrift store cardigan or hoodie
Layer 4: Vintage leather or denim jacket
Optional: All of the above simultaneously

Purpose:
- Pacific Northwest weather
- Hiding body (anti-sexy)
- Comfort and warmth
- Textural interest
- "I just grabbed whatever" look
```

### **Women's Grunge:**

**The Kinderwhore Look (Courtney Love):**
```
Contradictory Feminine:
- Baby doll dresses (vintage)
- Torn tights or fishnet
- Combat boots (Doc Martens)
- Smeared makeup
- Messy, dyed hair
- Mary Jane shoes sometimes
- Deliberate irony (cute + destroyed)
```

**The Riot Grrrl:**
```
Feminist Punk-Grunge:
- Band t-shirts (Bikini Kill, Bratmobile)
- Plaid mini skirts
- Ripped tights
- Combat boots
- DIY patches and pins
- Bold lipstick (red, dark)
- Written-on body (marker)
- Political statements
```

**Casual Grunge Girl:**
```
- Oversized flannel
- Band t-shirt
- Ripped boyfriend jeans
- Doc Martens or Converse
- Minimal makeup or none
- Messy hair (unwashed aesthetic)
- Thrift store cardigan
- Backpack
```

### **Men's Grunge:**

**Kurt Cobain Template:**
```
The Uniform:
- Striped long-sleeve shirt (under)
- Band t-shirt (over)
- Ripped jeans (light wash)
- Converse One Stars or Doc Martens
- Flannel (tied around waist or worn)
- Cardigan (grandpa-style)
- Greasy, unwashed blonde hair

Accessories:
- Sunglasses (oval, small)
- No effort to appearance
- Natural, unkempt
```

**Seattle Sound:**
```
- Thermal underwear tops
- Flannel (always)
- Baggy jeans or cords
- Work boots or Vans
- Beanie or no hat
- Facial hair (patchy, unkempt)
```

### **Color Palette:**

**Primary Grunge Colors:**
```
Dominant:
- Black (everything)
- Gray (heather, charcoal)
- Dark brown
- Olive green
- Dark burgundy
- Navy blue

Flannel Plaid:
- Red and black
- Blue and green
- Brown and tan
- Green and black
```

**Accent Colors:**
```
- Faded denim blue
- Mustard yellow
- Rust orange
- Forest green
- Muted jewel tones (worn-looking)
```

### **Footwear DNA:**

**Essential Shoes:**
```
Doc Martens:
- 1460 8-eye boot (most iconic)
- Black or oxblood
- Chunky, aggressive
- Initially worn-in look
- Yellow stitching visible

Converse:
- Chuck Taylor All-Stars
- Usually black or white
- High-top or low-top
- Worn, dirty
- Laces often untied or missing

Vans:
- Slip-ons or Old Skool
- Black and white checkerboard
- Skater influence
- Casual, easy

Combat Boots:
- Military surplus
- Black leather
- Heavy, chunky sole
- Authentic vintage preferred
```

### **Hair & Makeup:**

**Women:**
```
Hair:
- Unwashed, greasy look
- Center part
- Straight, long, unkempt
- Dyed (often badly): bleach blonde, black, burgundy
- Choppy, DIY cuts
- No styling products visible

Makeup:
- Smudged black eyeliner
- No foundation or minimal
- Dark lipstick (black, burgundy, brown)
- Pale skin (natural or powdered)
- Grunge: messy, not perfect
- Or completely bare face
```

**Men:**
```
Hair:
- Long, unwashed, greasy
- Center part or messy
- Blonde (often dyed badly)
- Shoulder-length or longer
- No product, natural
- Patchy facial hair

Grooming:
- Minimal to none
- Stubble or unkempt beard
- No cologne
- "Just woke up" aesthetic
```

### **Accessories:**

**Essential Items:**
```
- Backpack (JanSport or similar)
- Wallet chain
- Beanie (in any weather)
- Choker (black leather or velvet)
- Safety pins (functional or decorative)
- Patches (DIY, band logos)
- Pins/buttons (political, band)
```

### **Fabric Texture:**

**Preferred Materials:**
```
- Flannel (soft, worn cotton)
- Denim (faded, soft)
- Cotton jersey (t-shirts)
- Thermal waffle weave
- Corduroy (pants, jackets)
- Worn leather
- Wool (chunky knits)

Finish:
- Faded, vintage appearance
- Pilling acceptable
- Frayed edges
- Soft from many washes
- Never crisp or new-looking
```

### **High Fashion Grunge:**

**Marc Jacobs for Perry Ellis (1992):**
```
Controversy:
- Grunge on runway (scandal)
- Jacobs fired after show
- $800 flannel shirts
- Contradiction of grunge ethos
- Beanie and Doc Martens on models
- Commodification of anti-fashion

Impact:
- Brought grunge mainstream
- Legitimized "anti-fashion"
- Retail versions appeared
- Diluted authentic grunge
```

### **Cultural Context:**

**Music Foundation:**
- Nirvana "Nevermind" (1991)
- Pearl Jam "Ten" (1991)
- Soundgarden, Alice in Chains
- Sub Pop Records
- Seattle music scene
- Alternative rock explosion

**Social Context:**
- Generation X alienation
- Economic recession (early 90s)
- Anti-corporate sentiment
- Slacker culture
- Authenticity obsession
- Thrift store necessity/aesthetic

**Fashion Icons:**
- Kurt Cobain (ultimate grunge)
- Courtney Love (kinderwhore)
- Eddie Vedder (Pearl Jam)
- Kathleen Hanna (Riot Grrrl)
- Winona Ryder (Reality Bites)

**The Death of Grunge:**
- Kurt Cobain's death (1994)
- Mainstream co-optation
- "Grunge" in shopping malls
- Loss of authenticity
- Transition to other styles
