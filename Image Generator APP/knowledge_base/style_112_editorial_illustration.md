# STYLE 112: EDITORIAL ILLUSTRATION

## Overview
Conceptual art for magazines and newspapers that visualizes abstract ideas, commentary, and complex topics through striking visual metaphors. Combines artistry with journalism.

## Key Characteristics
- **Strong Conceptual Metaphors**: Visual idea communication
- **Bold Simplified Shapes**: Clear, impactful forms
- **Limited Color Palettes**: Intentional restriction
- **Striking Compositions**: Memorable layouts
- **Clear Visual Messaging**: Instant comprehension
- **Symbolic Imagery**: Metaphorical representation
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Current issues
- **Professional Polish**: Magazine-quality finish

## Essential Keywords
`editorial illustration`, `conceptual art`, `magazine illustration`, `opinion illustration`, `visual commentary`, `political cartoon art`, `newspaper illustration`, `contemporary editorial art`

## Prompt Templates

### Basic
"[concept] as editorial illustration, bold shapes, limited palette, strong metaphor"

### Enhanced
"[concept] as editorial illustration, striking visual metaphor, bold simplified geometric shapes, limited color palette of 3-4 colors, clear messaging, contemporary magazine quality, symbolic composition"

### Professional
"[concept] as professional editorial illustration for [publication type], powerful visual metaphor communicating [message], bold geometric simplified shapes, restricted color palette [specify colors], striking asymmetrical composition, symbolic imagery, clear instant readability, contemporary editorial art quality, suitable for magazine cover or feature spread"

## Technical Parameters
- **Simplification**: Reduce to essential elements
- **Color Count**: 2-5 colors maximum
- **Symbolism**: Universal recognizable symbols
- **Composition**: Dynamic, attention-grabbing
- **Scale**: Bold shapes, strong hierarchy

## Color Palette
- **Spot Colors**: 2-3 specific hues
- **Black/White**: High contrast base
- **Brand Colors**: Publication palette
- **Psychological**: Colors chosen for meaning
- **Printing**: CMYK or spot color considerations

## Best Use Cases
- Magazine covers, Op-ed pages, Feature articles, Political commentary, Business publications, Social issues, Technology topics, Cultural critique

## Example Prompts
"Climate change as editorial illustration, melting earth as ice cream cone metaphor, bold simplified shapes, limited palette of blue green and brown, striking composition, New Yorker magazine quality"

"AI and human relationship as editorial illustration, symbolic human and robot hands touching, geometric simplified forms, limited palette of blue and orange, contemporary tech magazine cover art, clear visual message"
