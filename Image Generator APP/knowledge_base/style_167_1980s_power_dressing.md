# 167. 1980s POWER DRESSING

### **Core Characteristics:**
- **Silhouette**: Broad shoulders, defined waist, strong lines
- **Aesthetic**: Bold, confident, excessive, status-driven
- **Philosophy**: Dress for success, power through appearance
- **Innovation**: Mixing masculine and feminine power

### **The Power Suit DNA:**

**Women's Power Suit:**
```
Structure:
- Shoulder pads (2-6 inches)
- Boxy, oversized jacket
- Nipped waist or belted
- Knee-length pencil skirt or trousers
- Double-breasted or single
- Strong, geometric silhouette

Visual Impact:
- Inverted triangle shape
- Masculine authority + feminine form
- Corporate warrior aesthetic
- Intimidating presence
- Status symbol
```

**Men's Power Suit:**
```
Structure:
- Wide shoulder padding
- Double-breasted preferred
- Peak lapels (wide)
- Pleated trousers (fuller cut)
- Bold pinstripes or solids
- Power tie (wide, bold)

Wall Street Look:
- Navy or charcoal with chalk stripes
- Suspenders (braces)
- French cuff shirts
- Bold geometric ties
- Wingtip shoes
- Gordon Gekko aesthetic
```

### **The Shoulder Pad Revolution:**

**Size Evolution:**
```
Early 80s (1980-83):
- Moderate padding (1-2 inches)
- Natural shoulder expansion

Mid 80s (1984-86):
- Dramatic padding (3-4 inches)
- Strong, square shoulders
- Peak power shoulder era

Late 80s (1987-89):
- Extreme padding (4-6 inches)
- Almost cartoonish proportions
- Maximum impact
```

**Placement Strategy:**
- Extends shoulder line outward
- Creates V-shape torso
- Balances wider hips
- Projects confidence
- Occupies more space
