# 162. EDWARDIAN ERA (1901-1914)

### **Core Characteristics:**
- **Silhouette**: S-bend corset creating "pigeon breast" profile
- **Aesthetic**: Romantic, feminine, "Gibson Girl" ideal
- **Innovation**: Simpler construction than Victorian, more movement
- **Color Shift**: Lighter, softer palettes, pastels dominant

### **Women's Fashion DNA:**

**The S-Bend Silhouette:**
```
Physical Structure:
- Straight-front corset pushes bust forward
- Hips thrust backward
- Creates dramatic S-curve profile
- Mono-bosom effect (shelf-like)
- Waist appears smaller through posture

Visual Impact:
- Elegant, swan-like neck
- Proud chest carriage
- Graceful backward lean
- Feminine, delicate appearance
```

**Daywear Standards:**
```
Blouses (Shirtwaists):
- High boned collars
- Lace jabots
- Tuck-pleated fronts
- Bell or bishop sleeves
- Light cotton or silk

Skirts:
- Floor-length, A-line
- Slight train for formal
- Gored construction (fitted hips, flared bottom)
- Often in darker colors than blouses

Accessories:
- Picture hats (large, decorated)
- Leather gloves
- Parasols
- Chatelaine bags
```

**Evening Wear:**
```
Gowns:
- Low necklines (rounded or square)
- Short puffed or cap sleeves
- Empire waistlines emerge
- Trained skirts for balls
- Delicate fabrics (chiffon, tulle, silk)

Embellishment:
- Lace overlays
- Beaded embroidery
- Silk flowers
- Feather trim
- Rhinestone or pearl details
```

### **Men's Fashion DNA:**

**Daily Wear:**
```
Three-Piece Suits:
- Sack coat (straight, loose fit)
- Waistcoat (matching or contrasting)
- Trousers (creased, cuffed)
- High, stiff collars
- Silk ties

Fabrics:
- Wool tweed (country wear)
- Worsted wool (business)
- Linen (summer)
- Patterns: pinstripes, herringbone, checks
```

**Formal Wear:**
```
Morning Dress:
- Morning coat (cutaway front)
- Striped trousers
- Waistcoat
- Ascot or four-in-hand tie
- Top hat

Evening Dress:
- Black tailcoat
- White piqué waistcoat
- White bow tie
- Patent leather shoes
- Opera hat (collapsible)
```

### **Color Psychology:**

**Women's Palette:**
- Soft pastels: powder blue, rose pink, lavender, mint
- Ivory and cream dominance
- Pale yellow, peach, seafoam
- White for summer and young women
- Deeper tones for winter: burgundy, forest, navy

**Men's Palette:**
- Gray (light to dark)
- Brown (tan to chocolate)
- Navy blue
- Black (formal occasions)
- White or cream shirts exclusively

### **Fabric Technology:**

**Revolutionary Materials:**
- Chiffon (lightweight, sheer, floaty)
- Georgette (crinkled chiffon)
- Silk satin (high sheen)
- Batiste (fine cotton)
- Voile (semi-sheer cotton)

**Trim Innovation:**
- Irish crochet lace (dimensional)
- Valenciennes lace (fine, delicate)
- Machine-made lace (affordable)
- Silk ribbon embroidery
- Cutwork and eyelet

### **Historical Context Notes:**

**Social Changes:**
- Women's suffrage movement growing
- Sports becoming acceptable (tennis, cycling)
- Automobile influence on fashion (dusters, veils)
- Tea gowns for informal entertaining
- Simplified dress reform beginning

**Technological Impact:**
- Electric lighting changes color choices
- Photography influences fashion documentation
- Department stores democratize fashion
- Fashion magazines proliferate
- Ready-to-wear industry grows
