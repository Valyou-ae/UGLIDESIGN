# 163. ROARING TWENTIES (1920-1929)

### **Core Characteristics:**
- **Silhouette Revolution**: Boyish, dropped waist, straight up-and-down
- **Length**: Hemlines rise to knee (scandalous!)
- **Aesthetic**: Art Deco, geometric, jazz age glamour
- **Liberation**: Corsets abandoned, movement prioritized

### **The Flapper DNA:**

**Physical Silhouette:**
```
Body Shape:
- Flat chest (binding popular)
- No defined waist
- Straight hips
- Boyish, androgynous figure
- Dropped waistline (hip-level)

Visual Impact:
- Rectangular silhouette
- Emphasis on legs
- Movement and dance-friendly
- Youthful, rebellious energy
```

**Iconic Flapper Elements:**
```
Dresses:
- Straight, loose fit
- Dropped waist (1926-28 lowest point)
- Knee-length hemlines
- Sleeveless or cap sleeves
- Fringe, beading, sequins

The Little Black Dress:
- Coco Chanel, 1926
- Simple, elegant, versatile
- Cocktail length
- Minimal ornamentation
- Revolutionary concept
```

### **Embellishment Techniques:**

**Art Deco Ornamentation:**
```
Beadwork:
- Geometric patterns
- Sunburst motifs
- Egyptian revival designs
- Zigzag and chevron
- All-over beading for evening

Sequins:
- Disc-shaped, reflective
- Sewn in patterns
- Creates shimmer in motion
- Gold, silver, iridescent

Fringe:
- Multiple tiers
- Beaded ends
- Movement emphasis
- Dance-activated design
```

**Surface Decoration:**
```
Embroidery:
- Metallic threads
- Geometric designs
- Art Deco motifs
- Chinese influence

Appliqué:
- Fabric cutouts
- Lace panels
- Contrasting textures
- Dimensional effects
```

### **Color Palettes:**

**Daywear:**
- Beige, cream, tan
- Soft gray, dove
- Pastel: powder blue, rose, mint
- Brown, navy (practical)

**Evening Wear:**
- Black (revolutionary)
- Metallic: gold, silver, bronze
- Deep jewel tones: emerald, sapphire, ruby
- White (summer, resort)

**Accent Colors:**
- Coral, peach
- Jade green
- Violet, purple
- Turquoise

### **Men's Fashion DNA:**

**The Jazz Age Gentleman:**
```
Suits:
- Wide-leg trousers ("Oxford bags")
- Double-breasted jackets
- Peak lapels
- Waistcoats (often contrasting)
- Pinstripes popular

Casual Innovation:
- Sweater vests
- Knit polo shirts
- Plus-fours (golf knickers)
- Spectator shoes (two-tone)
```

**Formal Wear:**
```
White Tie:
- Tailcoat
- White piqué waistcoat
- Wing collar
- White bow tie

Black Tie (emerging):
- Dinner jacket (tuxedo)
- Black or midnight blue
- Shawl or peaked lapel
- Black bow tie
```

### **Accessories Revolution:**

**Women:**
```
Head:
- Cloche hats (close-fitting, bell-shaped)
- Headbands (beaded, jeweled)
- Feather fascinators
- Short hair (bob, marcel wave)

Jewelry:
- Long pearl necklaces (rope length)
- Geometric brooches
- Art Deco rings
- Egyptian revival pieces
- Cigarette holders

Footwear:
- T-strap heels
- Mary Janes
- Low heels (1-2 inches)
- Open-toe sandals
```

**Men:**
```
- Fedora hats
- Panama hats (summer)
- Pocket squares
- Tie pins and clips
- Cufflinks (Art Deco design)
- Two-tone shoes
```

### **Fabric Innovation:**

**New Materials:**
- Rayon (first synthetic, "artificial silk")
- Jersey knit (Chanel's favorite)
- Lamé (metallic fabric)
- Crepe de chine
- Silk charmeuse

**Texture Play:**
- Velvet (evening)
- Satin (luxurious sheen)
- Chiffon (layers, floating)
- Georgette (soft drape)

### **Cultural Context:**

**The Jazz Age Spirit:**
- Post-WWI liberation
- Prohibition (speakeasies, cocktail culture)
- Women's suffrage achieved (1920)
- Economic boom (until 1929)
- Youth rebellion against Victorian values

**Fashion Influencers:**
- Coco Chanel (simplicity, jersey, little black dress)
- Jean Patou (sportswear)
- Elsa Schiaparelli (surrealist touches)
- Paul Poiret (exotic influence)

**Activities Driving Fashion:**
- Dancing (Charleston, Lindy Hop)
- Automobile touring
- Sports (tennis, golf)
- Cocktail parties
- Cinema going
