# 164. 1950s AMERICANA

### **Core Characteristics:**
- **Silhouette**: Hourglass figure, full skirts, cinched waist
- **Aesthetic**: Ultra-feminine, glamorous, wholesome
- **Cultural Impact**: Post-war optimism, suburban ideal
- **Color**: Bright, cheerful, saturated pastels

### **Women's Fashion DNA:**

**The New Look (Christian Dior, 1947-1950s):**
```
Silhouette Structure:
- Nipped waist (18-22 inches ideal)
- Full bust (padded, pointed bras)
- Full skirt (petticoats, crinolines)
- Soft, rounded shoulders
- Midi length (mid-calf)

Visual Impact:
- Extreme femininity
- Hourglass exaggeration
- Ladylike, refined
- Conservative yet glamorous
```

**Daywear Styles:**
```
Shirtwaist Dress:
- Button-front bodice
- Fitted waist with belt
- Full skirt
- Collar (peter pan or pointed)
- Short or 3/4 sleeves

Pencil Skirt Set:
- High-waisted, fitted skirt
- Below-knee length
- Kick pleat for walking
- Matching cardigan or blouse
- Structured, tailored

Full Circle Skirt:
- Gathered or circular cut
- Multiple layers petticoats
- Below-knee to mid-calf
- Novelty prints popular
- Poodle skirts (teenage)
```

**Evening Glamour:**
```
Cocktail Dresses:
- Tea-length (mid-calf)
- Fitted bodice, full skirt
- Luxurious fabrics
- Often strapless or cap sleeve
- Dramatic entrance-making

Ball Gowns:
- Floor-length, very full skirts
- Tulle, taffeta, satin
- Boned bodices
- Off-shoulder or sweetheart neckline
- White gloves required
```

### **Color Theory 1950s:**

**Primary Palette:**
```
Pastels (dominant):
- Powder blue
- Baby pink
- Mint green
- Butter yellow
- Lavender

Saturated Brights:
- Cherry red
- Turquoise
- Coral
- Emerald green
- Royal blue
```

**Novelty Print Patterns:**
- Polka dots (quintessential)
- Gingham checks
- Florals (large and small scale)
- Atomic/starburst motifs
- Novelty prints (poodles, cherries, kitchen items)

### **Men's Fashion DNA:**

**Business Uniform:**
```
The Gray Flannel Suit:
- Single-breasted, 2-button
- Natural shoulder
- Narrow lapels (compared to 1940s)
- Slim, tapered trousers
- Fedora hat

Shirt & Tie:
- White or light colored shirts
- Stiff collars
- Skinny ties (late 50s)
- Conservative patterns
- Tie clips
```

**Casual Revolution:**
```
Sportswear:
- Polo shirts (knit cotton)
- Chinos or khakis
- Cardigan sweaters
- Letterman jackets
- Penny loafers

The Greaser Look:
- White t-shirt
- Leather jacket
- Blue jeans (Levi's)
- Slicked-back hair
- Motorcycle boots
```

### **Teenage/Youth Culture:**

**Bobby Soxers:**
```
Girls:
- Full skirts with petticoats
- Cardigan sweaters (buttoned backwards)
- White bobby socks
- Saddle shoes
- Ponytails with ribbons
```

**Juvenile Delinquent Style:**
```
Boys:
- T-shirts (white or striped)
- Denim jeans (cuffed)
- Leather jackets
- Engineer boots
- Pompadour hairstyle
```

### **Fabric & Construction:**

**Key Materials:**
```
Cottons:
- Piqué (textured, structured)
- Broadcloth (smooth, crisp)
- Gingham (woven checks)
- Organdy (sheer, stiff)

Luxury Fabrics:
- Taffeta (rustling, structured)
- Tulle (netting for volume)
- Satin (high sheen)
- Velvet (evening wear)

New Synthetics:
- Nylon (stockings, lingerie)
- Orlon (acrylic)
- Dacron polyester
- Wash-and-wear fabrics
```

**Construction Details:**
- Boning in bodices
- Steel or horsehair crinolines
- Padded bras (pointed, "bullet" shape)
- Tailoring with structure
- Hand-finished details

### **Accessories Essential:**

Jewelry:

Headwear:
