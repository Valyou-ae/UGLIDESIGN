# 166. 1970s DISCO

### **Core Characteristics:**
- **Silhouette**: Body-conscious, flared, exaggerated
- **Aesthetic**: Glamorous, hedonistic, maximalist
- **Movement**: Dance-optimized, fluid fabrics
- **Shine**: Metallic, sequins, glitter everything

### **The Disco DNA:**

**Early 70s (1970-1975): Transition**
```
- Bell-bottoms emerging
- Platform shoes rising
- Maxi and midi lengths
- Ethnic influences
- Earth tones dominant
- Peasant styles
```

**Peak Disco (1976-1979): Saturday Night Fever**
```
Core Elements:
- Polyester everything
- Tight on top, flared on bottom
- Platform shoes (3-5 inches)
- Jumpsuit dominance
- Halter tops
- Wide collars
- Metallic fabrics
- Shine and sparkle
```

### **Women's Disco Fashion:**

**The Iconic Pieces:**
```
Wrap Dress (Diane von Furstenberg):
- Jersey knit
- Adjustable wrap tie
- V-neckline
- Midi length
- Bold prints
- Easy movement

Halter Jumpsuit:
- Bare back or shoulders
- Fitted bodice
- Wide-leg pants
- High waist
- Metallic or solid

Hot Pants Ensemble:
- Ultra-short shorts
- Matching vest or crop top
- Platform boots
- Often metallic or sequined
```

**Fabric & Embellishment:**
```
Materials:
- Polyester (double knit)
- Spandex/Lycra blends
- Lurex (metallic threads)
- Liquid lamé
- Qiana nylon (silky synthetic)

Surface Treatment:
- All-over sequins
- Mirror ball effect
- Metallic coating
- Glitter application
- Rhinestone details
```

### **Men's Disco Fashion:**

**The Disco King Look:**
```
Three-Piece Leisure Suit:
- Polyester blend
- Wide lapels (4-6 inches)
- Matching vest
- Flared trousers
- White, pastels, or patterns

Shirt DNA:
- Wide collar (butterfly or pointed)
- Deep V-neck (chest hair visible)
- Satin or silk-look polyester
- Often unbuttoned several buttons
- Body-conscious fit

The Iconic Disco Shirt:
- Shiny fabrics
- Bold prints (geometric, floral)
- Fitted through torso
- Long pointed collar
- French cuffs
```

**The John Travolta Effect:**
```
Saturday Night Fever (1977):
- White three-piece suit
- Black shirt

- Gold chain

- Open collar

**Women:**
```

- Platform sandals (ankle strap)
- Platform boots (knee-high)

- Disco roller skates

Heights:

Materials:

### **Jumpsuit Science:**

**Women's Jumpsuit:**
```
Construction:
- One-piece garment
- Fitted bodice
- Wide-leg pants or flared
- Often halter or strapless
- Zipper front or back

Variations:
- Catsuit (tight all over)
- Wide-leg palazzo
- Culotte style

**Men's Jumpsuit:**
```
- V-neck or zip front

- Flared legs

- Chest hair display

### **Cultural Context:**

**The Disco Movement:**
- Studio 54 (NYC epicenter)
- Underground clubs to mainstream
- LGBTQ+ safe spaces
- Racial integration on dance floors
- Cocaine culture
- Hedonism and escapism
- Economic anxiety backdrop

**Music Influence:**
- Bee Gees, Donna Summer
- Chic, Sister Sledge
- KC and the Sunshine Band
- Gloria Gaynor
- Four-on-the-floor beat
- String sections, synthesizers

**Fashion Icons:**
- Cher (Bob Mackie gowns)
- Diana Ross (glamorous gowns)
- John Travolta (white suit)
- Farrah Fawcett (feathered hair)
- Bianca Jagger (Studio 54 queen)
